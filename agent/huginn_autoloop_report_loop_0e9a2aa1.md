## Introduction

The accurate prediction of the silicon band gap remains a fundamental benchmark for density functional theory (DFT). While LDA and GGA functionals are widely used for structural properties, they are known to systematically underestimate band gaps—GGA typically by 30–50% [2]. The scientific question addressed here is whether the difference between LDA and GGA band gaps for Si can be attributed primarily to their differing predictions of the lattice constant, rather than to intrinsic electronic structure errors. This matters because isolating the structural contribution could guide functional selection and correction strategies without costly hybrid or GW calculations [1, 3].

The predicted band gap shift was then compared to the reported LDA vs. GGA band gap difference for Si.

This suggests that the majority of the LDA vs. GGA band gap discrepancy for Si originates from the lattice constant change, not from differences in exchange-correlation treatment of the electronic structure.
