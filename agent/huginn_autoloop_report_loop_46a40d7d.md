A key methodological question is whether pseudopotential approximations—which replace core electrons with effective potentials—introduce systematic errors compared to computationally more expensive all-electron methods. This study tests the hypothesis that oxygen vacancy formation energetics differ systematically between these approaches due to frozen-core approximations failing to capture full charge redistribution around the vacancy site.
