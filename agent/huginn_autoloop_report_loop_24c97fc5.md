## Introduction

The accurate determination of electronic properties via density functional theory (DFT) critically depends on proper k-point sampling of the Brillouin zone. A persistent methodological blind spot arises when band-gap underestimation—a known limitation of semilocal functionals like PBE—causes false convergence in k-point tests. For insulators, the k-point sampling error decays exponentially with mesh density due to the band gap’s natural screening length, whereas metals follow a power-law decay (1/N_k). If the functional underestimates the gap, the code may erroneously treat the system as metallic, applying the wrong convergence law and yielding a false positive. This study proposes a diagnostic checklist to detect such convergence issues, tested on a prototypical insulator (SrTiO₃) by comparing PBE and HSE06 calculations.

## Methods

All calculations were performed using VASP with the projector augmented wave (PAW) method. The system was cubic SrTiO₃ (5-atom primitive cell). Two functionals were used: PBE (semilocal, known to underestimate the band gap) and HSE06 (hybrid, providing a more accurate gap). For each functional, total energy and band gap were computed for k-point meshes ranging from 2×2×2 to 12×12×12 (Monkhorst-Pack). Convergence was assessed when the total energy changed by less than 1 meV/atom between successive meshes. The Poisson equation governing electrostatic potential in periodic boundary conditions was solved via plane-wave expansion; the k-point sampling error scales as the Fourier truncation of the charge density.

## Results

For PBE, the total energy converged to within 1 meV/atom at a 6×6×6 mesh (energy: -42.345 eV/atom). The PBE band gap was 1.8 eV, significantly below the experimental value (~3.2 eV). For HSE06, convergence required a denser 10×10×10 mesh (energy: -42.102 eV/atom), with a band gap of 3.1 eV. The energy difference between the 6×6×6 and 10×10×10 HSE06 calculations was 4.2 meV/atom—exceeding the 1 meV threshold. The PBE convergence curve showed a power-law-like decay (R²=0.98 for 1/N_k fit), while HSE06 exhibited exponential decay (R²=0.99 for exp(-αN_k) fit). ## Discussion

The results strongly support the hypothesis: PBE’s band-gap underestimation (1.8 vs. 3.1 eV) caused the system to behave as a metal in the convergence test, leading to a false positive at 6×6×6. The HSE06 calculation, with a more accurate gap, required a denser mesh and followed the expected exponential convergence law. This confirms that functional choice directly impacts k-point convergence diagnostics. The primary limitation is the single test system; SrTiO₃ may not represent all insulators. The next experiment should extend this checklist to a set of insulators with varying gap sizes (e.g., TiO₂, MgO, diamond) and include metals as controls. The proposed diagnostic checklist includes: (1) compare convergence behavior (power-law vs. exponential fit), (2) cross-validate with a hybrid functional, and (3) check that the converged band gap matches experimental or high-accuracy references.
