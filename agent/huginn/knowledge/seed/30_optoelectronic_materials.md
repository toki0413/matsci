# Optoelectronic Materials

## Key Properties
- **Band gap**: Determines absorption/emission wavelength; direct gaps preferred for LEDs and lasers.
- **Carrier mobility**: Governs device speed and transport-layer design.
- **Exciton binding energy**: Important for organics and 2D materials; impacts radiative recombination efficiency.

## Classes of Materials
- **Inorganic semiconductors**: GaAs, GaN, InP, perovskites (halide and oxide).
- **Organic semiconductors**: Small molecules (Alq₃, TPBi) and conjugated polymers (PEDOT:PSS, P3HT).
- **Two-dimensional materials**: Transition-metal dichalcogenides (MoS₂, WSe₂) for valleytronics and flexible optoelectronics.

## Computational Methods
- **GW / BSE**: Accurate quasiparticle gaps and optical spectra.
- **TDDFT**: Excitation energies and oscillator strengths for molecules and solids.
- **Non-radiative recombination**: Defect-mediated Shockley–Read–Hall recombination rates.

## Device Considerations
- Align energy levels at interfaces to reduce injection barriers.
- Account for spin–orbit coupling in heavy-element systems.
- Optimize charge balance and exciton confinement in active layers.
