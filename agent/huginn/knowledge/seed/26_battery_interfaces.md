# Battery Interfaces and Interphases

## Solid–Electrolyte Interphase (SEI)
- A passivation layer formed by electrolyte reduction on the anode.
- Main components: Li₂CO₃, LiF, ROCOOLi, etc., depending on electrolyte salts/solvents.
- Controls lithium-ion transport, electronic leakage, and cycling stability.

## Cathode–Electrolyte Interphase (CEI)
- Oxidation products on cathode surfaces at high potentials.
- Thickness and composition depend on voltage window and electrolyte additives.

## Interface Stability Metrics
- **Electrochemical window**: Voltage range where electrolyte is thermodynamically stable.
- **Interfacial impedance**: From EIS; related to ionic conductivity and layer thickness.
- **Wetting**: Contact angle and work of adhesion between electrode and electrolyte.

## Modeling Strategies
- **DFT slabs**: Study adsorption and decomposition of solvent/salt molecules.
- **Ab-initio MD**: Probe interfacial structure and Li⁺ solvation at finite temperature.
- **Continuum / equivalent-circuit models**: Fit EIS spectra to extract resistances.

## Best Practices
- Compare interfacial energies of candidate SEI components.
- Use explicit solvent models when studying reduction/oxidation mechanisms.
- Validate predicted windows against cyclic voltammetry where possible.
