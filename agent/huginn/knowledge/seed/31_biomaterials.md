# Biomaterials

## Design Criteria
- **Biocompatibility**: No adverse immune or toxic response in the host.
- **Biodegradability**: Controlled degradation rate matching tissue regeneration.
- **Mechanical matching**: Young’s modulus close to native tissue to avoid stress shielding.
- **Surface properties**: Wettability, roughness, and functional groups direct cell adhesion.

## Common Classes
- **Metals**: Titanium and its alloys for orthopedic implants; Mg-based biodegradable metals.
- **Ceramics**: Hydroxyapatite, bioactive glass for bone regeneration.
- **Polymers**: PCL, PLGA, PEG, collagen, chitosan for scaffolds and drug delivery.
- **Composites**: Polymer–ceramic hybrids combining elasticity and osteoconductivity.

## Modeling and Simulation
- **Molecular dynamics**: Protein–surface interactions, hydration layers, peptide adsorption.
- **Finite element analysis**: Mechanical behavior of implants and scaffolds under physiological loading.
- **Agent-based / cellular Potts models**: Cell migration and tissue growth on scaffolds.

## Experimental Validation
- In vitro cytotoxicity (ISO 10993-5), degradation in simulated body fluid, mechanical testing after immersion.
