# Scanning Probe Microscopy (SPM)

## Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM)
- Measures topography via forces between a sharp tip and sample surface.
- Modes: contact, tapping/intermittent contact, non-contact.
- Resolution: lateral ~few nm, vertical < 0.1 nm for ideal samples.

## Scanning Tunneling Microscopy (STM)
- Requires conductive samples; measures tunneling current between tip and surface.
- Can achieve atomic resolution on flat, clean surfaces.
- Bias voltage determines whether occupied or unoccupied states are probed.

## Derived Measurements
- **KPFM**: Kelvin probe force microscopy maps surface potential / work function.
- **MFM**: Magnetic force microscopy images magnetic domains.
- **PFM**: Piezoresponse force microscopy for ferroelectric domains.
- **AFM-IR**: Infrared AFM for chemical identification with nanoscale resolution.

## Artifacts and Best Practices
- Tip convolution can broaden features; use deconvolution or ultra-sharp tips.
- Mechanical drift and scanner non-linearity require calibration on standards.
- Avoid excessive force in contact mode to prevent sample damage.
