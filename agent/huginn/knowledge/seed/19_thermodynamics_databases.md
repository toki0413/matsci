# Thermodynamics Databases and Resources

## Calphad Databases
- **Thermo-Calc / TC**: Commercial databases for steels, Ni-based superalloys, Al alloys, oxides, etc.
- **FactSage**: Gibbs energy minimizer with databases for slags, salts, and aqueous systems.
- **OpenCalphad**: Open-source CALPHAD engine for thermodynamic assessments.

## Materials Property Repositories
- **NIST-JANAF Thermochemical Tables**: Standard thermodynamic data for inorganic substances.
- **NIST Materials Data Repository**: Curated experimental data for materials.
- **MatNavi / NIMS**: Japanese materials database with mechanical, physical, and thermodynamic properties.
- **Aflowlib / Materials Project**: High-throughput computed formation energies and phase diagrams.

## Phase Diagram Resources
- **ASM Alloy Phase Diagram Database**: Experimentally assessed binary and ternary diagrams.
- **MPDS / Springer Materials**: Comprehensive phase-diagram and crystal-structure data.
- **AFLOW-CHULL**: Online convex-hull diagrams from ab-initio calculations.

## Best Practices
- Always cite the database version and reference states used.
- For computed data, check convergence and functional approximations.
- Combine CALPHAD with key experiments (DSC, DTA, XRD) to validate predictions.
