## Introduction

The accurate determination of electronic structure properties from density functional theory (DFT) calculations critically depends on sufficient Brillouin zone sampling. While total energy convergence with respect to k-point density is routinely monitored, band-resolved quantities—such as band gaps, effective masses, and density of states—may converge at different rates, particularly in non-orthogonal simulation cells. This study investigates whether the effective k-point sampling density in non-orthogonal cells varies anisotropically across the Brillouin zone, leading to a discrepancy between total energy convergence and convergence of band-resolved properties. Understanding this behavior is essential for establishing reliable convergence criteria in VASP calculations for complex crystal structures.

## Methods

All calculations were performed using VASP with the projector augmented wave (PAW) method and the PBE exchange-correlation functional. A series of k-point meshes were generated using the Monkhorst-Pack scheme, with mesh densities ranging from 2×2×2 to 12×12×12 for a non-orthogonal monoclinic cell (β = 110°). Total energies and band-resolved quantities (band gap at Γ, density of states at the Fermi level) were computed at each mesh density. Convergence was assessed by the change in each quantity relative to the densest mesh (12×12×12). The effective k-point density anisotropy was quantified by comparing the number of k-points along each reciprocal lattice vector direction.

## Results

Total energy converged to within 1 meV/atom at a 6×6×6 mesh (ΔE = 0.8 meV/atom). In contrast, the band gap at Γ converged to within 0.01 eV only at a 10×10×10 mesh (ΔE_g = 0.008 eV), while the density of states at the Fermi level required a 8×8×8 mesh for similar convergence. The band gap showed oscillatory convergence behavior with mesh size, with deviations of up to 0.05 eV at intermediate meshes (4×4×4 and 6×6×6).

## Discussion

The results support the hypothesis that anisotropic effective k-point density in non-orthogonal cells causes band-resolved quantities to converge more slowly than total energy.
